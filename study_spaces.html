Study Spaces

The University has a great selection of study spot on Central Campus. Whether you need a quiet spot to do homework or hold a study group, here is a list of places for your studying needs:

  • Michigan Union
  • Shapiro Library
  • The Fishbowl

Michigan Union

The Union is one of the most popular study spots for students! Conveniently located on State Street, the Union offers 4 floors of study spaces and nice bathrooms. The basement holds many open study spots and a food court for Panda Express, Subway, and Qdoba. If you are in need to IT services, the Michigan Tech Shop has a location in the basement. On the first floor, the Union is usually busy, offering opening seating outside of the Sweetwaters and Panera Bread if you want a quick bite to eat. Student events and guided tours reguarly happen here so it is usually busy year around. If you need a super quiet place to get some work done, there is quiet study lounge just for that by the entrance.

Shapiro Library (Ugli)

The Shapiro Library, or more commonly known as the Ugli, is one of the most popular libraries on central campus. Located at the heart of campus by the Diag, the Ugli has 4 floors of open study spaces, private rooms, computer labs, and a pleathora of charging spots. With a cafe situated at the entrance, you can grab a coffee or a snack when studying. The third floor is newly renovated and offers many comfortable seating. Make sure to come early as the third floor usually gets very busy.

The Fishbowl

The Fishbowl is located within Mason Hall. Open 24/7, this is a popular destination for students looking to have a late-night study session. During all times of the day, you can usually find a seat to study here. There are computers and chargers at each seat. It is most busy in the last afternoons and evenings as many student come here after class to study individually or with friends. It can get quite loud, so be sure to bring headphones if you prefer a quiet environment.
